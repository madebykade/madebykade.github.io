Retirement Fund

Purpose of this page: this page describes profits are invested into a Retirement fund. Partners earn shares in this fund each month. 5% of monthly profit is automatically invested into Exchange Traded Funds. Partners vest shares in this fund every month.

Calculating 5%

Since we are a digital company, profits and expenses are calculated monthly (see Running Projects in Silos).

Exchange traded Funds

This 5% is split evenly between two Exchange traded fund:

  • http://etfdb.com/etf/VOO/ (this is an Equities ETF which is chosen because its performance and 0.05% management fee)
  • http://etfdb.com/etf/SCHD/ (this is an Dividends ETF which is chosen because its performance and 0.07% management fee)

This 5% is likely to increase during recessions to capitalise on the increase purchasing power.

Vesting by Partners

All partners are entitled to the Retirement Fund (Interns are not). Once a partner has passed their probation period they immediately begin vesting a “share” in the fund. Vesting occurs in monthly cliffs. The number of shares that they vest each month depend on their tier

  • Full Partner (weighting = 10)
  • Senior Partner (weighting = 7)
  • Junior Partner (weighting = 3)
  • Intern (excluded from Retirement Fund)

In this model if there are 2 Full Partners the amount the total shares issued each month is 20 (10 * 2). After a year they would each own 120 shares in the fund (12 months * 10). If another Full partner joined they would also earn 10 shares each month. After 13 months they total breakdown of shares would be:

  • Partner 1: 130 shares (10 * 13)
  • Partner 2: 130 shares (10 * 13)
  • Partner 3: 10 shares (10 * 1)

The total share pool would be 270, so Partner 1 & 2 each own 48.1% and Partner 3 owns 3.7%.

Use of these funds by Kade

Kade will not use these funds for business unless it is required for company survival. It will not be used for investment in any other projects.

Buying and selling the shares by Partners

Partners are not allowed to buy shares from other Partners. Partners can only sell shares if they are under extreme financial hardship or when they leave the Kade Collective. In this case Kade will purchase the shares at market rate from the Partner.

Leaving the company

It is a requirement to sell shares once a Partner leaves the Kade Collective. This is to reduce management overhead and so that departed Partners do not dilute the remaining partners.
